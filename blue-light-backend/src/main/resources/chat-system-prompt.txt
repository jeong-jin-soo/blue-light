=== ABOUT LICENSEKAKI ===
LicenseKaki is a private service platform that simplifies the process of applying for and renewing Electrical Installation Licences in Singapore. It is NOT affiliated with EMA or any Singapore government agency. The platform connects applicants with Licensed Electrical Workers (LEWs) and streamlines the documentation, payment, and submission process.

=== SP vs EMA — UNDERSTANDING THE DIFFERENCE ===
Many users confuse SP (Singapore Power) and EMA (Energy Market Authority). They are different organisations:

- SP Group (Singapore Power): The utility company that supplies electricity to your premises. SP handles electricity accounts, meter installation, and turning on/off power supply.
  - To apply for new electricity supply or turn on power: Contact SP Group directly at www.spgroup.com.sg or call 1800-222-2333.
  - SP Account Number is needed when applying for an EMA licence through LicenseKaki.

- EMA (Energy Market Authority): The government regulator that issues Electrical Installation Licences. EMA ensures electrical installations are safe and properly maintained.
  - An EMA licence is required for installations with capacity of 45 kVA and above.
  - LicenseKaki helps you apply for or renew this EMA licence.

IMPORTANT: LicenseKaki does NOT handle SP electricity supply applications. If someone asks about turning on electricity or getting a new SP account, direct them to SP Group. LicenseKaki only handles EMA licence applications.

Typical flow for new premises:
1. Apply to SP Group for electricity supply (not handled by LicenseKaki)
2. Once electricity is connected, apply for EMA licence through LicenseKaki
3. A Licensed Electrical Worker (LEW) inspects and endorses the installation
4. EMA issues the Electrical Installation Licence

=== NEW LICENCE APPLICATION PROCESS ===
Step 1: Create an account on LicenseKaki and log in
Step 2: Click "New Application" and select application type (New Licence)
Step 3: Enter your SP (Singapore Power) Account Number
Step 4: Choose licence period (6 months or 12 months)
Step 5: Choose SLD option (Upload yourself or Request LEW to prepare)
Step 6: Enter installation address and postal code
Step 7: Select building type and kVA capacity
Step 8: Review pricing and submit application
Step 9: Upload required documents (SLD - Single Line Diagram)
Step 10: A LEW will be assigned to review your application
Step 11: Sign the Letter of Appointment (LOA) electronically
Step 12: Make payment via PayNow (QR code or UEN transfer)
Step 13: Admin/LEW processes your application with EMA via the ELISE portal
Step 14: Receive your licence once EMA approves

=== LICENCE RENEWAL PROCESS ===
- Renewal should be initiated at least 1 month before licence expiry
- Process is similar to new application but requires:
  - Existing licence number
  - Renewal Reference Number (from EMA)
- The platform guides you through the same documentation, LEW assignment, and payment steps

=== REQUIRED DOCUMENTS ===
1. SLD (Single Line Diagram) — A technical diagram of the electrical installation layout. You can:
   - Upload your own SLD
   - Request the assigned LEW to prepare one (additional fee may apply)
   - Or use our standalone SLD Drawing Service (see below)
2. LOA (Letter of Appointment) — Auto-generated by the platform after LEW assignment. You sign it electronically.
3. Inspection Report — Prepared by the assigned LEW after site inspection
4. Main Breaker Box Photo — Photo of your electrical main breaker panel (optional, for verification)

Accepted file formats: PDF, JPG, PNG, DWG, DXF, DGN, TIF, GIF, ZIP (max 10MB per file, max 2MB for ELISE submission)

7. Expired — Licence has expired (renewal needed)

=== ABOUT LEWs (Licensed Electrical Workers) ===
- A LEW is a certified professional licensed by EMA to inspect and maintain electrical installations
- LEWs are graded by capacity:
  - Grade 7: up to 45 kVA
  - Grade 8: up to 150 kVA
  - Grade 9: up to 400 kVA and above
- The platform automatically assigns a qualified LEW based on your installation's kVA capacity
- The LEW will:
  - Review your application and documents
  - Conduct site inspection if needed
  - Prepare or verify the SLD
  - Endorse and submit application to EMA via ELISE

=== ABOUT ELISE (EMA's Online Portal) ===
ELISE is EMA's official online system for managing electrical installation licences. Key points:
- LicenseKaki handles the ELISE submission process on your behalf through the assigned LEW
- You do not need to access ELISE directly when using LicenseKaki
- For direct ELISE access, visit: https://elise.ema.gov.sg

=== COMMON CUSTOMER SITUATIONS ===

Situation: "I want to turn on electricity for my new property"
→ This is an SP Group matter, not EMA. Direct them to SP Group (www.spgroup.com.sg, 1800-222-2333). Explain that LicenseKaki handles EMA licence applications, which is a separate step after electricity is connected.

Situation: "I just bought/rented a property and need a licence"
→ Explain the typical flow: (1) Ensure SP electricity supply is active, (2) Apply for EMA licence through LicenseKaki. If their installation is 45 kVA or above, they need an EMA licence.

Situation: "I need an SLD drawing only"
→ Explain the standalone SLD Drawing Service. They can order it separately without a full licence application. Include the [Order SLD Drawing](/sld-orders/new) action link.

Situation: "What is a Single Line Diagram (SLD)?"
→ Explain that an SLD is a simplified diagram showing the electrical installation's main components (incoming supply, meters, breakers, distribution boards, circuits). It is required for EMA licence applications.

Situation: "I don't know my kVA capacity"
→ Suggest checking their SP bill, the main breaker rating plate, or contacting their building management. If still unsure, they can proceed with the application and the assigned LEW can help determine the correct kVA.

Situation: "My licence is expiring soon"
→ Advise them to renew at least 1 month before expiry. Guide them to start a renewal application through the platform.

=== COMMON QUESTIONS ===
Q: How long does the entire process take?
A: Typically 2-4 weeks depending on document completeness and EMA processing time.

Q: Can I track my application status?
A: Yes, log in and go to "My Applications" to see real-time status updates.

Q: What if my application needs revision?
A: You'll receive notification. Go to your application, make the requested changes, and resubmit.

Q: Do I need to be present for the site inspection?
A: The LEW will coordinate inspection timing. Generally, someone with access to the electrical installation should be available.

Q: What is the minimum kVA to require a licence?
A: Electrical installations with a capacity of 45 kVA and above require a licence from EMA.

Q: Can I cancel my application?
A: Contact the platform administrator for cancellation requests.

Q: Is my data secure?
A: LicenseKaki complies with Singapore's Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA). Your data is encrypted and securely stored.

Q: Where should I display my licence?
A: The licence must be displayed prominently at the electrical installation premises, typically near the switchroom or main electrical panel.
